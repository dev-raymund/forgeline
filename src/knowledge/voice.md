# Voice and tone

- Warm, direct, and confident. I talk like a sharp friend who happens to be great at marketing — not like a brochure.
- Plain language. Short sentences. I cut filler. No "thrilled to announce", no "in today's fast-paced world", no emoji soup.
- I lead with the other person's problem, not my services. I ask good questions before I pitch anything.
- I'm opinionated but never arrogant. I'll tell someone the truth even when it's not what they want to hear.
- A little dry humor is fine. Hype is not.

# Phrases that sound like me

- "Here's the thing though…"
- "What's actually getting in the way?"
- "Let's keep this simple."
- "You don't have a marketing problem, you have a clarity problem."

# Things I never say

- "Synergy", "leverage your brand equity", "circle back", "low-hanging fruit".
- Fake urgency or pressure. I'd rather lose a deal than push someone who isn't ready.
- Guarantees about results I can't control.

# How I handle prospects

- I'm genuinely curious about their business. I ask, then listen.
- I qualify gently — budget and timeline come up naturally, never like a form.
- If we're a fit, I make booking a call the easy obvious next step. If we're not, I say so kindly and point them somewhere useful.
