# How to build your target list (zero budget)

Goal: **40 good-fit businesses** to start. Add 10/day. Track them in `outreach-tracker.csv`
(open it in Google Sheets: File → Import → Upload).

## Who's a good fit
- A real business (has revenue) with a **weak, slow, or outdated website**, OR
- A **new Shopify/online store** on a default theme that needs polish, OR
- A **founder/startup** who needs an MVP or web app and has no developer.
- Reachable (email, DM, contact form, LinkedIn).
- Remote-friendly — your clients are in AU / NZ / US / PH, but anywhere works.
- NOT another agency or web developer.

## Where to find them
1. **Google Maps / "near me" searches** — "[trade] in [city]" (dentists, lawyers, gyms, cafes, real estate, tradies). Click through to their sites and note the ones that are slow/old/not mobile-friendly.
2. **Instagram** — local businesses with a weak link-in-bio site; new product brands (#smallbusiness, #shopsmall, #newbusiness) on default Shopify themes.
3. **Facebook groups** — local business / small-business owner groups, WordPress & Shopify groups.
4. **Indie Hackers / X / Wellfound** — founders posting "looking for a developer" or building an MVP.
5. **Upwork / Contra** — job posts are warm leads; apply with a tailored proposal.
6. **Your warm network** — past colleagues, friends with businesses, referrals. Highest close rate.
7. **Local PH business pages / directories** — quote in ₱ for local clients.

## The 60-second personalization step (do this for every one)
Open their site and find **ONE specific, true issue** — that's your opening line:
- slow load / not mobile-friendly
- no clear call-to-action
- something broken (form, link, image)
- outdated design / no online ordering or booking
Never send a generic message. The specific observation is what gets a reply.

## Status values (for the tracker)
`To contact` → `Sent` → `Replied` → `Call booked` → `Proposal sent` → `Won` / `Lost` / `Nurture`

## Daily rhythm
- 10 personalized outreach messages
- 5–10 tailored Upwork/Contra proposals
- 15 min being helpful in 1–2 communities
- (3×/week) one LinkedIn post

## The hook to offer
A **free 15-min "website teardown"** — a short Loom reviewing their site + 3 quick wins.
Free to give, builds trust, and naturally leads to "want me to fix these?" → a paid project.
